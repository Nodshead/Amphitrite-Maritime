What Amphitrite is

Ocean Bulletin is Amphitrite's maritime intelligence platform. It combines high-resolution ocean currents, waves, and wind — 3.6 km resolution, hourly updates, 10-day forecast — into a single operational picture for planning, routing, and offshore decision-making.

It is not a weather dashboard. It is not currents-only. It is the data layer that connects metocean conditions to operational decisions — available as a browser platform or as an API feed into your existing tools.

Why currents matter

Most marine operations teams already use wind and wave forecasts. The gap is currents — and it is a significant one.

Standard government ocean models (EU Copernicus, NOAA) see the ocean in roughly 10 km blocks. Eddies, coastal currents, tidal interactions, and current meanders — the features that actually push vessels off course, spike fuel consumption, and affect offshore operability — get smeared out or missed entirely.

In surveys of captains and marine operations teams, 70% rate current forecasts as average or poor. Wind data is generally fine. Current data is the persistent weak link.

Amphitrite's edge is making currents usable in real operational decisions: 3.6 km resolution, AI-fused from three independent satellite sources, validated against 165,000 independent buoy measurements. Our forecasts are correct to within 45° of real current direction 76% of the time, compared to ~52% for standard models. Dangerously wrong forecasts (more than 90° off) drop from roughly 1-in-4 to less than 1-in-12.

How we get our data
1
Satellite inputs. Sea surface height (SWOT & nadir altimetry), sea surface temperature (infrared), and chlorophyll concentration — fused from global observation networks.
2
Processed through HIRES-CURRENTS — Amphitrite's proprietary deep-learning AI, built to reconstruct ocean dynamics at high resolution. Developed from CNRS and École Polytechnique research.
3
Validated against in-situ measurements. 165,000 independent buoy and drifter observations — not used in training, used only as ground truth. Peer-reviewed methodology, published in Remote Sensing (2024).
4
Output: currents, waves, and wind at 3.6 km resolution — 10-day operational forecast, hourly intervals, global coverage. Available as platform or API.

Common questions
Do we still need specialist weather routing tools?
+

Amphitrite fits alongside specialist tools, not instead of them. It is the high-resolution current and metocean data layer that most routing platforms either lack or receive at low resolution. Teams that already use StormGeo, PredictWind, or similar services typically find Amphitrite improves the current input quality rather than replacing the routing workflow.

Is this only for shipping?
+

No. The platform is used across any marine operation where currents, waves, and wind affect decisions — offshore construction, subsea, survey, rig moves, port operations, and project cargo. The data is the same; the operational thresholds and use cases differ by application.

Can this fit into our existing workflow?
+

Yes. Ocean Bulletin is browser-based — no installation, no hardware. Waypoints export in GPX and CSV formats that load into any navigation system. For deeper integration, the API delivers JSON output into your own tools. We start with a pilot to show the data in the context of your actual operation before any commitment.

Do you cover offshore and coastal areas?
+

Yes. Global coverage from 60°S to 65°N latitude, including shelf seas, coastal approaches, and straits. Tidal models are integrated alongside ocean currents — the English Channel, Strait of Gibraltar, and other critical chokepoints have dedicated high-resolution tidal layers.

Can we start with a pilot before committing?
+

That is exactly how we start. A route study, a short operational trial, or platform access for a specific voyage or project — we work with you to show the data in your context before any longer engagement. There is no obligation at the pilot stage.
