What it is

The Ocean Bulletin — maritime intelligence platform.

Amphitrite is a maritime weather intelligence platform combining high-resolution ocean currents, waves, and wind into one operational picture. Available as a browser-based platform (Ocean Bulletin) or as a data API for integration into your systems.

1
3.0 km resolution — hourly wind, wave, and ocean current data at a horizontal scale that resolves coastal eddies, tidal jets, and current boundaries that standard models miss entirely.
2
HIRES-CURRENTS — Amphitrite's proprietary deep-learning AI current model, developed with CNRS and École Polytechnique. Trained on 165,000+ independent in-situ buoy measurements. 14-day forecast horizon, global coverage.
3
Platform or API — plan routes interactively in Ocean Bulletin, or pull structured data directly into your voyage management system, ETA tools, or operational dashboards via the MetOcean API.
4
Hindcast, reanalysis, and forecast — historical data back years for post-voyage analysis and route learning; real-time and forecast data for operational planning. One unified data source.

The data gap

Wind and wave forecasting is mature. Ocean current forecasting hasn't reached the same standard — until now.

Wind forecasting has achieved near-operational accuracy at 1/4° resolution globally. Wave models follow directly from wind. But ocean surface currents are a fundamentally different problem: eddies are an order of magnitude smaller than atmospheric storms, and there are far fewer ocean measurements than atmospheric ones.

70% of ship captains rate ocean current forecasts as average or poor — compared to just 4% for wind. That gap is what Amphitrite closes. (Source: captain survey, Amphitrite white paper, April 2026.)

Standard operational models (EU Copernicus, US NOAA) often disagree significantly on surface currents — predicting different locations and intensities for the same current vein on the same day. Misplacing a current vein by 30–50 nautical miles changes the routing decision entirely.

HIRES-CURRENTS accuracy vs EU & US operational models — validated against 165,000+ independent in-situ measurements across North Atlantic and South Africa:

→
37% of forecasts excellent (direction error <15°) vs 23% for EU and US operational models
→

Operational use cases

Where it fits into maritime operations.

Route optimisation — all three variables
Optimise passage plans against wind, waves, and ocean currents at 3.0 km. Plan routes that exploit favourable currents, avoid counter-currents, and select weather windows — all in one platform.
Offshore workability windows
DSV, crane vessel, and construction vessel ops depend on wave height, period, and current speed. Get accurate go/no-go decisions days in advance — not just wind-based estimates.
DP-sensitive operations
Accurate ocean current data at the operational site reduces thruster demand, improves DP station-keeping, and extends operational weather windows — particularly in areas with strong tidal or eddy currents.
Tow and rig move planning
Tug-and-tow operations are highly sensitive to current forces across the towline. Forecast currents at 3.0 km along the entire route, not just at waypoints — critical for loaded semi-sub and jacket tows.
Port approach and coastal passages
High-resolution tidal and coastal current data for approaches to Gibraltar, the English Channel, and major chokepoints — where standard models degrade to unusable resolution.
Heavy cargo and project shipping
Combined current, wave, and wind forecast for scheduling departure windows, planning sailing angles, and reducing exposure to adverse conditions on high-value, low-margin transits.
Fuel and emissions management
Current-informed routing reduces fuel consumption directly — both by exploiting favourable currents and avoiding headwinds created by counter-current conditions. Validated fuel savings from AIS data on real voyages.
Voyage risk reduction and ETA accuracy
14-day forecast at 3.0 km improves ETA confidence, reduces unexpected delays, and gives fleet managers a consistent, accurate picture across the fleet — not dependent on individual ship's officer judgment.
How the data is built

From satellite to operational forecast in one pipeline.

1
Observation ingestion — satellite altimetry (SWOT + nadir), SST from infrared satellites, chlorophyll, 165,000+ in-situ drifter and buoy measurements across North Atlantic and South Africa.
2
HIRES-CURRENTS — proprietary deep-learning AI — trained end-to-end on observational data. Resolves mesoscale eddies, current veins, and tidal signatures that numerical ocean models miss. 3.0 km horizontal resolution. (Garcia et al., AIES 2025; Kugusheva et al., Remote Sens. 2024.)
3
Wind and wave integration — ECMWF IFS (1/4°, hourly) for wind; ECMWF WAM and ERA5 for wave; regional tidal models (FES2014 global; AMM15 for English Channel; Puertos del Estado for Gibraltar). All variables at common resolution.
4
14-day forecast output — wind, waves, and ocean currents at 3.0 km resolution, updated hourly. Available via Ocean Bulletin platform or MetOcean API in multiple formats compatible with major navigation and VMS systems.
Custom vessel model

Where accuracy becomes personal to your vessel.

Generic vessel type assumptions introduce systematic routing error. A custom vessel model removes that error by learning how your specific vessel responds to every combination of wind angle, wave direction, and current — using your actual voyage history.

→
Position logs, speed through water, speed over ground, heading, COG — from your AIS and onboard sensors
→
Roll and pitch data, hull dimensions, engine power curves — from vessel records
→
Output: a performance polar diagram — speed gain or loss at every wind angle, wave direction, and current strength
→
Result: the platform knows exactly how your vessel responds to conditions — not a generic assumption

Better routing leads to more accurate ETAs, better fuel prediction, and routing decisions that account for your vessel's actual handling characteristics under load.
